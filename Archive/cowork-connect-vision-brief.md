# CoWork Connect
## Product Vision Brief

---

### The Problem

Remote workers, freelancers, and independent professionals often struggle with isolation and lack of accountability. While co-working spaces exist, they don't solve the social connection problem—people sit in the same room but rarely collaborate or even talk. There's no easy way to find someone in your city who wants to work alongside you on a given day, share focus time, or simply have a coffee break conversation about your respective projects.

---

### The Vision

**CoWork Connect** is where productivity meets community. A location-based social app that helps people find co-working partners based on what they're working on, when they're available, and where they want to work—whether that's a café, library, park, or home office video call.

Think of it as Tinder for co-working: swipe through profiles of people nearby who want to work together, see what project or task they're tackling today, and match to meet up or join a virtual session.

---

### Core Value Proposition

**For the user:** "Never work alone again. Find your people, stay accountable, and make work social."

**For the market:** The first app that treats co-working as a social experience rather than just a real estate solution.

---

### Target Users

| Segment | Description | Key Need |
|---------|-------------|----------|

### Core Features

#### 1. Discovery (Swipe Interface)

The heart of the app. Users see cards of people nearby who are available to co-work.

**Each card shows:**
- Profile photo and name
- Current project/task ("Building a Figma plugin" or "Writing my thesis")
- Work style tags (e.g., "Deep focus," "Happy to chat," "Pomodoro fan")
- Preferred location type (café, library, home video call)
- Availability window ("Today 2-6pm")
- Distance from you

**Actions:**
- Swipe right → interested in co-working
- Swipe left → pass
- Tap card → view full profile
- Super swipe → send a direct message with your swipe

**Matching:** When two people swipe right, they match and can message to coordinate.

---

#### 2. Profiles

**Basic info:**
- Name, photo, short bio
- City / neighborhood
- Profession or field
- Links (portfolio, LinkedIn, Twitter—optional)

**Work preferences:**
- Typical availability (recurring schedule)
- Preferred session length
- Work style (focused silence vs. casual chat)
- Favorite spots in the city

**Today's intent:**
- What you're working on today
- When you're available
- Where you'd like to work

---

#### 3. Friends & Connections

Not every co-work partner becomes a regular. The friends feature lets you build your personal co-working circle.

**Features:**
- Add friends after a successful session
- See when friends are "working now" or available
- Quick invite: tap a friend to propose a session
- Friend activity feed (optional): "Alex is working at Blue Bottle until 5pm"

---

#### 4. Group Chats & Communities

For people who want ongoing co-working communities rather than one-off matches.

**Types of groups:**
- **Location-based:** "Williamsburg Remote Workers"
- **Interest-based:** "Founders Building in Public"
- **Schedule-based:** "Early Birds Club (6-9am)"

**Group features:**
- Group chat for coordination
- Shared calendar of group sessions
- "I'm heading to [location]" broadcast
- Group challenges (e.g., "Ship something this week")

**Discovery:** Browse or search for groups in your city; request to join or auto-join open groups.

---

#### 5. Sessions & Meetups

Structured co-working sessions that anyone can create or join.

**Session types:**
- **1:1 match sessions:** Result of a swipe match
- **Open sessions:** Public, anyone can join ("Working at Café Grumpy 2-5pm, 3 spots")
- **Group sessions:** Organized by a community
- **Virtual sessions:** Video call with optional screen share or ambient cam

**During a session:**
- Check-in: share your goal for the session
- Timer (optional Pomodoro support)
- Check-out: share what you accomplished
- Rate the session and your co-worker (optional, private)

### User Journey Example

1. **Sarah** downloads CoWork Connect. She's a freelance designer in Austin feeling isolated working from home.

2. She sets up her profile: "UX Designer | Currently redesigning my portfolio | Love café work, happy to chat between sprints."

3. She opens Discovery and sees **Marcus**, a developer 0.5 miles away: "Building a habit tracker app. Looking for afternoon focus session at a café."

4. Sarah swipes right. Marcus already swiped right on her. Match!

5. They message: "Cenote on East 6th at 2pm?" "Perfect, see you there."

6. They meet, work for 3 hours, and have a great conversation during breaks. Sarah adds Marcus as a friend.

7. A week later, Sarah joins the "Austin Creative Freelancers" group she found in Browse. She sees there's a group session Thursday at a new co-working café—she joins.

8. Over time, Sarah has a circle of 8 regular co-working friends and a community she taps into weekly. She's more productive and far less lonely.

### Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What they do | Our difference |
|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **WeWork / Spaces** | Physical co-working spaces | We're about people, not places |
| **Focusmate** | Virtual co-working via video | We enable in-person + have social features |
| **Lunchclub** | AI-matched networking calls | We're about working together, not just talking |
| **Bumble BFF** | Friend-finding via swipes | We're purpose-driven (productivity + accountability) |
| **Meetup** | Event-based communities | We're for everyday work, not just events |

**Our unique position:** The only app focused on spontaneous, location-based co-working with both discovery (swipes) and community (groups/friends).

### Success Metrics

**North Star Metric:** Weekly active co-working sessions per user

**Supporting metrics:**
- Matches per user per week
- Match → session conversion rate
- Repeat sessions with same person
- Friends added per user
- Group membership and activity
- Retention (D7, D30, D90)
- NPS (Net Promoter Score)

### Monetization (Future)

| Model | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Freemium** | Free tier with limited swipes/day; premium unlocks unlimited + features |
| **Premium features** | See who swiped on you, advanced filters, priority in Discovery |
| **Group subscriptions** | Paid communities with extra features (events, member directory) |
| **Partnerships** | Cafés and co-working spaces pay for visibility or to host sessions |

### MVP Scope

For initial launch, focus on:

1. ✅ User profiles with "today's work intent"
2. ✅ Swipe-based discovery (location + availability)
3. ✅ Matching and basic messaging
4. ✅ Add friends after session
5. ✅ Simple session check-in/check-out

**Defer to v2:**
- Group chats and communities
- Virtual co-working sessions
- Gamification and challenges
- Premium features

---

### Open Questions

1. **Safety:** How do we handle meeting strangers? Verification, ratings, block/report features?
2. **Gender dynamics:** Do we need Bumble-style controls for who can message first?
3. **Cold start:** How do we seed enough users in a city to make Discovery useful?
4. **Habit formation:** What triggers bring users back daily?
5. **Virtual vs. IRL:** What's the right balance? Should we start with one?
