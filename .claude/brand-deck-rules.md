# Clover Brand Deck Rules
*Instructions for anyone creating a Clover presentation from scratch.*

---

## 1. Visual Identity

### Colors

| Role | Name | Hex |
|------|------|-----|
| Screen / slide background | Soft Lavender | `#ede8ff` |
| Primary text, buttons, headings | Forest Green | `#1e3d28` |
| Accents, taglines, links | Violet | `#7c5cbf` |
| Dividers, subtle backgrounds | Muted Lavender | `#d0c8f0` |
| Cards / surfaces | White | `#ffffff` |
| Secondary text | Muted Forest | `#3a4a3f` |

**Rules:**
- Never introduce new brand colors. Use only the six above.
- Forest green on lavender background is the default pairing.
- Violet is accent-only — never use it as a background for large areas.
- Dark slides (e.g. statement quotes) use Forest Green `#1e3d28` as the background with Muted Lavender `#d0c8f0` text.

### Typography

| Use | Font | Weight | Size guidance |
|-----|------|--------|---------------|
| Slide titles / hero quotes | Cormorant Garamond | Light (300) | 48–64px |
| Subheadings / card headers | Cormorant Garamond | Light (300) | 28–36px |
| Body text, bullets, labels | DM Sans | Light (300) | 15–20px |
| Buttons, bold callouts | DM Sans | Medium (500) | 14–16px |
| Decorative / italic emphasis | Cormorant Garamond Light Italic | 300 Italic | Match heading size |

**Rules:**
- Never use bold Cormorant — light weight is the brand voice.
- Never use DM Sans for titles. Serif is always the headline font.
- Cormorant italic is used for full-bleed quote slides only.
- Line height for headings: 1.15. Body: 1.7.

### Logo Usage
- **On light (lavender) backgrounds:** use the Forest Green `#1e3d28` wordmark.
- **On dark (forest green) backgrounds:** use the Muted Lavender `#d0c8f0` wordmark.
- Logo sits top-left on title slides. Omit from body slides unless required.
- The brand name is always written as **Clover** — never ALL CAPS, never lowercase.

## 2. Slide Structure

### Title Slide
1. Clover wordmark (top-left or centered)
2. Tagline: *"Find your people. Do the work."* — Cormorant italic, violet
3. Presentation title — Cormorant Light, forest green, large
4. Presenter name + date — DM Sans Light, small, muted

### Typical Deck Flow

| Section | Purpose | Slide types |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| **Opening** | Hook the audience emotionally | Full-bleed quote slide, founder story |
| **Problem** | Make the audience feel the pain | Two-column contrast, single statement |
| **Insight** | The "aha" that changes the frame | Dark statement slide, bullet list (max 3) |
| **Solution** | Introduce Clover | Logo reveal, one-liner, how-it-works steps |
| **Product** | Show the thing | Phone mockup frames, screen labels |
| **Traction** | Build credibility | Status list with checkmarks |
| **Close** | Drive action | CTA slide with QR code or link |

### Bullet Points
- **Maximum 3 bullets per slide.** If you have more, split into two slides.
- Use short fragments, not full sentences. Each bullet is ≤ 10 words.
- No nested bullets. Ever.

### Data & Charts
- Prefer simple bar or single-stat callouts over complex charts.
- Label all data points directly on the chart — no separate legends.
- Use Forest Green as the primary data color, Violet as the secondary.
- State the so-what in the slide title, not the chart title.
  - ✅ *"Builders work alone 4 days out of 5"*
  - ❌ *"Days per week working solo (survey, n=200)"*

### Sentence Style
- Headlines and titles: **short fragments**. No full stops.
- Body text when needed: full sentences, conversational, present tense.
- Never use passive voice. Write as if talking to the person in the room.

## 3. Tone and Language

### Voice
- **Warm and direct.** Not corporate. Not over-designed startup speak.
- Write like a smart friend explaining something they care deeply about.
- Aspirational but grounded — the vision is real, not hype.
- Never condescending. The audience are builders too.

### Words and Phrases We Use
- *builders* (not "users" or "customers")
- *co-work / coworking* (one word or hyphenated)
- *find your people*
- *set your intent*
- *work side by side*
- *meet IRL*
- *do the work*
- *accountability* (not "productivity hack")
- *connect* (not "network")

### Words and Phrases We Avoid
- "synergy", "leverage", "ecosystem", "disrupt"
- "users" — always say *builders*
- "scaling" as a standalone buzzword
- "seamless", "frictionless", "best-in-class"
- "social network" — Clover is a *discovery app*, not a social network

### Referring to Clover
- The app: **Clover** (capital C, no article — "open Clover", not "open the Clover")
- The company: **Clover** (same)
- The people using it: **builders**
- What they do: **co-work, connect, meet up**

## 4. Recurring Slide Elements

### Slide Counter
- Bottom-right corner, small, low opacity (e.g. `3 / 10`).
- DM Sans Light, same color as body text, ~55% opacity.

### Full-Bleed Quote Slides
- Used for emotional hooks — opening and closing the narrative arc.
- Dark background (`#1e3d28`), large Cormorant Light Italic, Muted Lavender text.
- No images, no bullets, no logo. Quote only.
- Reserve for the single most important sentence on that slide.

### CTA / Close Slide (always last)
- Headline: one imperative sentence. E.g. *"Be the first builders on Clover."*
- Large QR code linking to waitlist or App Store.
- URL in small DM Sans below the QR.
- Dark background (Forest Green).

### Contact / Team Slide
- Include if presenting to investors or new partners.
- Keep it minimal: name, role, email. No headshots unless essential.

### Disclaimers
- Not currently required for pitch demos.
- If used, place in a small footer (DM Sans Light, 10px, muted, bottom of slide).
